Remote Sensing

What is Remote Sensing?

The science of obtaining information about objects or areas from a distance, typically from aircraft or satellites.

Types of Sensors

Learn about optical, thermal, and microwave sensors and their applications in Earth observation.

Applications

Exploring Our Solar System

Planetary science is the scientific study of planets, moons, and planetary systems, both in our own solar system and in others. It includes understanding the formation, evolution, and current state of these celestial bodies.

Recent missions like NASA's Perseverance rover on Mars, ESA's Rosetta mission to comet 67P, and ISRO's Chandrayaan missions to the Moon have revolutionized our understanding of planetary bodies.

Understanding the Universe

Astrophysics seeks to understand the universe and our place in it. It studies the physics of celestial objects and phenomena including stars, galaxies, black holes, and the cosmic microwave background.

Breakthroughs like the detection of gravitational waves, imaging of black holes, and discoveries of exoplanets have opened new frontiers in our cosmic understanding.

Advancing Space Technology

Space technology encompasses spacecraft, satellites, space stations, and support infrastructure, equipment, and procedures. It's essential for space exploration, Earth observation, telecommunications, and more.

Innovations like reusable rockets, small satellites, and advanced propulsion systems are making space more accessible than ever before.

Notable Space Missions

1957

Sputnik 1

The first artificial Earth satellite launched by the Soviet Union, marking the beginning of the space age.

1969

Apollo 11

The first manned mission to land on the Moon, with Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin becoming the first humans to walk on the lunar surface.

1990

Hubble Space Telescope

Launched into low Earth orbit, Hubble has provided some of the most detailed images of distant galaxies and nebulae.

2021

James Webb Space Telescope

The most powerful space telescope ever built, designed to study every phase in the history of our Universe.
